About Me

My journey as a designer is driven by a core belief in the power of reconnecting with ourselves, the earth, and the energy that moves through all living beings. The spaces we inhabit, from the home to the landscape, should be a source of that connection. I find deep peace around animals and feel most fulfilled when I'm creating spaces that nurture connection, calm, and a sense of belonging.

My Interior Design degree taught me to create residential spaces that were tailored to everyone’s personality and needs. I learned that understanding space, movement, and the overall experience was essential, and it taught me empathy, adaptability, and how to design for real people with real needs. I felt a pull beyond the walls I was designing, which led me to become a Landscape Architect. Now, I aim to help people reconnect with nature in intentional, nourishing ways, making these experiences accessible with just a short walk.

As someone who left home to chase growth and create something meaningful, I know what it means to search for belonging. That’s why I design the way I do. I want every space to feel like home, not just in form, but in feeling where it feels safe, seen, and inspired by the presence of other living beings. I believe in energy, in meaningful connections, and in design that speaks to the soul.

My long-term dream is to build a self-sustaining sanctuary for animals, a place that offers them safety, dignity and love that heals them but also us. It's a vision that fuels my work today, for their future.
